# Productivity Rates -- Construction Output Norms

## Purpose
Productivity rates define HOW FAST work is done -- measured in m3/day, t/day, m2/day per gang. They are independent of cost (unit prices) and design (geometry). Rates are adjusted by climate and market factors.

## Duration Formula
**Duration (days) = Quantity / (Gang output/day x Number of gangs x Productivity factor)**

## Key Concepts
- **Base rate**: Output per gang per day under temperate conditions (1.00x factor)
- **Productivity factor**: Climate/location multiplier (e.g., 0.80x for Gulf)
- **Gang**: A crew of workers with defined composition (e.g., 1 foreman + 12 fixers)
- **Multiple gangs**: Parallel crews on large structures (up to 4-5 concurrent)

## Climate Adjustment Factors
| Zone | Factor | Key Constraint |
|------|--------|---------------|
| Temperate (baseline) | 1.00 | Standard 8-hr day |
| Hot arid (Gulf/Saudi) | 0.80 | Heat ban 1200-1500, split shift, night pours |
| Tropical (SE Asia) | 0.85 | Monsoon delays, humidity |
| Cold (Nordic/Canada) | 0.85 | Winter heating, shorter daylight |
| Extreme cold (<-20C) | 0.70 | Heated enclosures required |
