# Quantity Takeoff -- Measuring What Gets Built

## Purpose
Quantity takeoff converts design dimensions into measured quantities (m3, t, m2, lm). It bridges design-assumptions (geometry) and cost-bases (unit prices). Measurement rules define deduction/addition conventions.

## BOQ Structure
Standard 14-section bill of quantities:
1. Preliminaries | 2. Earthworks | 3. Blinding | 4. Base Slab | 5. Wall Concrete
6. Roof Concrete | 7. Reinforcement | 8. Formwork | 9. Waterproofing
10. Joints & Waterstops | 11. Backfill | 12. M&E/Piping | 13. Testing | 14. Sundries

## Key Conventions
- Concrete: net volume (no rebar deduction)
- Formwork: contact area (both faces for walls)
- Rebar: net cutting length + laps + wastage (5-8%)
- Earthworks: neat line + 1m working space each side
