# OPEX Benchmarks by Facility Type

## How to Use These Benchmarks

These are indicative ranges for budgetary purposes and order-of-magnitude validation. Actual OPEX depends heavily on:
- Local energy prices
- Labor market conditions
- Feedstock/chemical costs
- Technology selection
- Plant utilization rate
- Maintenance philosophy (preventive vs. reactive)
- Age of facility

Always calibrate against actual data from comparable operating facilities when available.

## General Industrial

### OPEX as Percentage of CAPEX (Rules of Thumb)

| Facility Type | Annual OPEX / TIC | Notes |
|--------------|-------------------|-------|
| Process plant (chemicals) | 8–15% | Higher for corrosive/hazardous |
| Power plant (thermal) | 3–6% | Fuel cost dominant, not included |
| Water treatment | 5–10% | Energy cost dominant |
| Mining (processing) | 10–20% | High wear, consumables |
| Oil & gas (upstream) | 5–10% | Varies with well count |
| Infrastructure (roads, bridges) | 1–3% | Low maintenance intensity |
| Buildings (commercial) | 3–6% | HVAC, cleaning, security |

## Staffing Benchmarks

### Operations Staffing (Indicative)
| Facility Type | Operators per Shift | Total Staff (5-shift) |
|--------------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| Small process plant (<$50M TIC) | 2–4 | 12–25 |
| Medium process plant ($50–200M) | 4–8 | 25–50 |
| Large process plant ($200M–1B) | 8–20 | 50–120 |
| Desalination (50,000 m³/d) | 3–5 | 20–35 |
| Power plant (CCGT, 400MW) | 3–5 | 20–35 |

### Maintenance Staffing (Indicative)
Rule of thumb: Maintenance staff ≈ 0.5–1.0 × operations staff for process plants.
