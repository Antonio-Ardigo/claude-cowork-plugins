# Testing & Commissioning

## 1. Visual Inspection (pre-test)
- Inspect joints for honeycombing, cold joints, visible cracks
- Check waterstop continuity at all construction joints
- Verify all penetrations sealed
- Mark defects for repair before filling

## 2. Hydrostatic Test
- Fill rate: max 2m/day
- Fill to overflow level (design water depth + freeboard)
- Hold period: 7 days minimum (EN 1992-3)
- Measure: twice daily (morning + evening)
- Correct for: evaporation, thermal expansion, rainfall

## 3. Acceptance (EN 1992-3)
- Tightness Class 1: max loss = 1/500 of water depth per day
  (e.g., 5.92m depth = max 11.8mm/day)
- Stabilization: first 3 days absorption allowed; measure final 4 days
- No visible leaks, damp patches, or weeping on external faces

## 4. Leak Repair
- Crack injection: epoxy or PU for cracks >0.2mm
- Surface sealing: crystalline coating for hairline <0.2mm
- Joint repair: cut out and re-seal failed waterstop
- Re-test: 7-day hold after repair

## 5. Disinfection (potable tanks)
- Chlorinate to 50 mg/l, hold 24 hours
- Drain, flush, refill
- Test: residual Cl <0.5 mg/l, bacteriological clear

## 6. Commissioning
- Test all valves (open/close cycles)
- Test instruments (level, alarms, quality analyzer)
- SCADA integration test (if applicable)
- Emergency procedures walk-through

## 7. Handover
- As-built drawings (within 30 days of PAC)
- Material certificates (concrete, rebar, waterstop, WP)
- Test reports (cubes, hydrostatic, chlorination)
- O&M manual
- Warranties: WP 10 years, M&E 2 years
- Defects liability: 12 months from PAC
