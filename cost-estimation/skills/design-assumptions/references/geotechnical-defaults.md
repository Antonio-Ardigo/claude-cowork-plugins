# Geotechnical Defaults

## Bearing Capacity Assumptions
| Soil Type | Default Bearing (kPa) | Settlement | Notes |
|-----------|----------------------|------------|-------|
| Dense sand/gravel | 200-300 | < 25mm | Ideal for tanks |
| Medium sand | 150-200 | 25-50mm | Typical default |
| Stiff clay | 100-200 | 25-75mm | Check long-term settlement |
| Soft clay | 50-100 | > 75mm | Ground improvement needed |
| Rock | > 500 | Negligible | Reduce excavation depth |

## Piling Triggers
- Bearing capacity < 100 kPa
- Variable ground across footprint (> 30% variation)
- High water table with uplift risk (empty tank check)
- Liquefiable soils in seismic zones

## Water Table
- Default assumption: 3m+ below formation level (no dewatering)
- Coastal sites: assume within 1-2m (dewatering required)
- Desert (Saudi inland): typically > 5m (minimal risk)
- Sabkha soils: saturated at shallow depth, aggressive to concrete

## Site Investigation Requirements
- Minimum: 1 borehole per 900 m2 for tanks > 10,000 m3
- Depth: 1.5x widest dimension below formation level
- In-situ testing: SPT, CPT, plate load test
- Laboratory: classification, strength, chemical analysis (sulfate, chloride)

## Ground Improvement Options
- Vibro-compaction (granular soils)
- Stone columns (soft clay)
- Jet grouting (variable ground)
- Dynamic compaction (fills)
- Subgrade modulus: 20-80 MN/m3 (sand), 5-30 MN/m3 (clay)
