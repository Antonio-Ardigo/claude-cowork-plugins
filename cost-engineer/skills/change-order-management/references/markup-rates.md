# Markup Rates by Contract Type and Region

## Typical Markup Structure

Markups are applied sequentially (compound), not stacked on the base:

```
Step 1: Direct Costs                           = Base
Step 2: + Overhead (on Direct)                 = Subtotal 1
Step 3: + Profit (on Subtotal 1)               = Subtotal 2
Step 4: + Bond (on Subtotal 2)                 = Subtotal 3
Step 5: + Insurance (on Subtotal 3)            = Total CO Value
```

## Overhead Rates

Overhead covers the contractor's home office costs, corporate G&A, and field office overhead not captured in direct costs.

| Contract Type | Overhead % | Basis |
|--------------|-----------|-------|
| Lump sum (EPC) | 8–12% | On direct costs |
| Unit rate / remeasurable | 10–15% | On direct costs |
| Cost-reimbursable | 5–8% | On direct costs (lower because more costs are reimbursed directly) |
| Time & materials | 10–15% | On labor costs |
| Construction management | 8–12% | On direct construction costs |

### Overhead Components
- Corporate G&A (rent, IT, HR, finance, legal)
- Regional office overhead
- Project-specific overhead not in general conditions
- Small tools and consumables (if not line-itemized)
- Quality assurance (corporate level)
- Estimating and bidding costs
- Warranty reserve

## Profit Rates

| Contract Type | Profit % | Basis |
|--------------|---------|-------|
| Lump sum (competitive bid) | 5–8% | On directs + overhead |
| Lump sum (negotiated) | 8–12% | On directs + overhead |
| Unit rate / remeasurable | 5–10% | On directs + overhead |
| Cost-reimbursable + fee | Fixed fee or 3–7% | On allowable costs |
| Design-build | 8–15% | On directs + overhead |

### Profit on Change Orders vs. Base Contract
- Some contracts specify a **different (lower) profit rate for changes** than the base bid
- Typical: base bid profit 10%, change order profit 5–8%
- Always check the contract's change clause for specified rates

## Bond Premium

Performance and payment bond premiums based on contract value:

| Contract Value | Bond Rate |
|---------------|-----------|
| <$1M | 2.0–3.0% |
| $1M–$5M | 1.5–2.5% |
| $5M–$25M | 1.0–2.0% |
| $25M–$100M | 0.75–1.5% |
| >$100M | 0.50–1.0% |

**Change order bond premium:** Applied as incremental premium on the change value, at the rate applicable to the cumulative contract value.

## Insurance

| Type | Rate | Basis |
|------|------|-------|
| General liability | 0.5–2.0% | On contract value |
| Builder's risk | 0.2–0.5% | On contract value |
| Workers' compensation | 3–15% | On labor payroll (varies by trade and jurisdiction) |
| Professional indemnity | 0.5–1.5% | On design fees |
| Contractor's all-risk (CAR) | 0.3–1.0% | On contract value |

**Note:** Workers' comp is typically included in the labor rate, not as a separate markup. Verify what's in vs. out of the labor rate before adding insurance markup.

## Self-Performed vs. Subcontracted Work

| Markup | Self-Performed | Subcontracted |
|--------|---------------|---------------|
| Overhead | Full rate | Reduced (5–8%) or none |
| Profit | Full rate | Reduced (3–5%) or none |
| Bond | Applicable | Pass-through only |
| Insurance | Applicable | Pass-through only |

Many contracts specify reduced or zero markup on subcontract pass-through costs, since the GC's overhead and profit contribution is lower for managed subcontracts.

## Contract Clause Checklist

Before pricing a change order, verify the contract for:

1. **Specified overhead and profit rates** for changes (often different from base bid)
2. **Whether markups apply to credits** (cost deletions) — some contracts apply no markup to credits
3. **Tier pricing** — different rates for self-performed vs. subcontracted work
4. **Cumulative change threshold** — some contracts cap total change value or adjust rates above a threshold
5. **Time-based markups** — extended overhead rate for schedule extensions
6. **Dispute resolution** — process if parties cannot agree on change value
7. **Documentation requirements** — what backup is required with the change order submission
